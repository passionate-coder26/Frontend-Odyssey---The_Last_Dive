# 🌊 The Last Dive — An Immersive Deep Sea Journey

> *"I've run from this long enough. Fear has had its turn. Now it's mine."*

## Project Description

Jacques Morel, 67, stands on a cliff edge above the Pacific. His daughter Sophie thinks he's retired. The world thinks he's done. He has one last dive to make — 11,000 meters straight down — to find a colleague who never surfaced.

**The Last Dive** is a cinematic, scroll-driven storytelling experience that takes you through five ocean zones, each darker and more dangerous than the last. It began as a question: *what if a website could feel like descending into the unknown?*

The design process started with atmosphere first. Color palettes were chosen to mirror real ocean optics — cerulean at the surface bleeding into abyssal black by 4,000 meters. Typography follows the emotional arc: serif for Jacques' inner voice, monospace for cold system readouts, and cinematic Cinzel for zone titles that feel carved from the seafloor itself.

As you scroll through the **Twilight Zone**, you find an abandoned oxygen tank — its valve forced shut from the outside. Deeper, in the **Midnight Zone**, a shredded submarine hull bears marks no pressure could leave. In the **Abyss**, a crushed helmet holds a note: *"It's not a monster. It's beautiful."*

By the **Hadal Zone**, you're 11,000 meters down. A single eye opens in the dark. Jacques reaches for a photograph of Sophie. And something ancient — something that has been waiting — *understands*.

The entire experience is built with **React + Framer Motion + Three.js**, with a custom torch-cursor that turns your mouse into the only light source. Every clue is interactive, every zone is a chapter, and the ending is yours to earn.
